# Competitor Analysis & Service Structure Recommendations
### Aurelius Media — March 2025

---

## Executive Summary

This document analyzes three leading digital marketing agencies — **WebFX**, **Disruptive Advertising**, and **Thrive Agency** — with a focus on how they structure their services menu, individual service pages, and blog content. The goal is to inform a revised service architecture and page template for Aurelius Media.

**Key finding:** Disruptive Advertising's "What We Do" navigation model is the best fit for Aurelius — clean, intent-based categories that map directly to client outcomes. Their structure, adapted with Aurelius's unique AI positioning, forms the basis of the recommendations below.

### 1.1 WebFX

**Scale:** 750+ staff · $10B+ in client revenue generated · $3B+ in client sales tracked · 7.8M+ leads delivered · 1,000+ testimonials

#### Service Categories & Menu Structure

WebFX uses a broad, comprehensive menu organized into seven major pillars:

| Category | Key Services |
|---|---|
| **SEO** | National SEO, Local SEO, Ecommerce SEO, Enterprise SEO, GEO (AI Search Optimization) |
| **PPC** | Google Ads, Bing Ads, Display Advertising, Remarketing |
| **Social Media** | Facebook Ads, Instagram Ads, LinkedIn, Twitter, Pinterest |
| **Content Marketing** | Blog writing, SEO content, copywriting |
| **Email Marketing** | Campaign management, automation, lead nurture |
| **Web Design** | Website design, redesign, landing pages |
| **CRO** | A/B testing, user research, funnel optimization |
| **Amazon** | Amazon SEO, Amazon PPC |
| **Marketing Operations** | Analytics, reporting, RevenueCloudFX (proprietary platform), CRM integration |

WebFX also has deep industry vertical pages (healthcare, finance, home services, manufacturing, etc.) but these are separate from the core service nav.

#### Individual Service Page Anatomy

WebFX service pages are long-form and data-heavy. Structure in order:

1. **Hero** — Bold headline with revenue/growth promise, primary CTA ("Get a Free Proposal"), secondary CTA
2. **Social Proof Bar** — Agency-level stats (e.g., "$10B+ in revenue generated," "93% client retention," number of 5-star reviews)
3. **Services Overview** — What's included in this service, feature grid
4. **How It Works / Our Process** — Numbered steps (typically 4–5), icon-driven
5. **Pricing Plans** — Transparent tiered pricing table (Aggressive → Market Leader → Trailblazer) with feature breakdown
6. **Results / Case Studies** — Data-specific wins (e.g., "+236% organic traffic for Client X")
7. **Why WebFX** — Agency differentiators, team credentials, proprietary tech (RevenueCloudFX)
8. **Testimonials** — Multiple client quotes with name, company, industry
9. **FAQ** — Accordion format, 6–10 questions
10. **Final CTA** — Proposal form embed or calendar link

**Standout patterns:**
- **Transparent pricing** is WebFX's biggest differentiator — most competitors hide pricing. This builds trust immediately.
- **RevenueCloudFX** is mentioned throughout every page, reinforcing the proprietary tech advantage.
- Stats are agency-level ("$10B+ generated") AND service-level ("SEO clients see 3x more traffic on average"), creating dual social proof.
- Every page has a live chat widget and a sticky "Get a Proposal" CTA in the header.

#### Blog Structure

Blog is organized by primary discipline:
- **SEO** — keyword research, technical SEO, local SEO tips
- **PPC** — Google Ads, bidding strategies, campaign optimization
- **Social Media** — platform-specific tactics, algorithm updates
- **Content Marketing** — content strategy, writing guides
- **Email Marketing** — automation, segmentation, deliverability
- **Web Design** — UX, conversion design, mobile optimization
- **Analytics & Reporting** — GA4, attribution, ROI measurement
- **Marketing Technology** — AI tools, CRM, marketing platforms

Posts are typically 1,500–3,000 words, SEO-optimized with clear H2/H3 structure, internal links to service pages, and a CTA at the bottom. Heavy on "ultimate guides" and "X best practices for [year]" formats.

### 1.3 Disruptive Advertising ⭐ (Most Relevant Reference)

**Scale:** 150+ full-time employees · $450M+ in annual ad spend managed · #1 most 5-star reviewed PPC agency on Clutch

#### Service Categories & Menu Structure

Disruptive's "What We Do" navigation is the strongest example of clean, outcome-oriented service categorization. Their menu uses exactly 6–7 top-level categories with clear sub-services under each:

| Category | Sub-Services |
|---|---|
| **Paid Search** | Google Ads, Microsoft Ads, Google Shopping, Retargeting, YouTube Ads, PPC Management |
| **Paid Social** | Facebook & Instagram Ads, LinkedIn Ads, TikTok Ads, Snapchat Ads, Twitter/X Ads |
| **SEO** | (Standalone — full-service technical + content) |
| **CRO** | eCommerce CRO, Landing Page Testing, Lead Gen CRO |
| **Creative** | Landing Page Design, Ad Creative Strategy, Video & Motion |
| **Lead Generation** | (Full-funnel demand gen, multi-channel) |
| **Lifecycle Marketing** | Email Marketing, Lead Nurture |
| **Data & Analytics** | Attribution, reporting, analytics setup |

**Why this works so well:**
- Categories map directly to client intent ("I want to run Google Ads" → Paid Search)
- Not overcrowded — 7 categories is the right density for a mega-menu
- Creative is elevated to its own category (most agencies bury it under "services")
- Lifecycle Marketing separates retention from acquisition — shows strategic maturity

#### Individual Service Page Anatomy

Disruptive's service pages feel like a direct conversation with a prospect. Structure:

1. **Hero** — Strong outcome headline ("Stop Wasting Your Google Ad Budget"), subheading, two CTAs: "Get a Free Audit" (primary) + "See Our Work" (secondary)
2. **Social Proof Bar** — Award badges, review platform ratings, spend managed stat
3. **The Problem We Solve** — Empathetic problem framing, bullet list of pain points the prospect likely has
4. **Our Approach** — Methodology overview, what makes their process different
5. **Services Breakdown** — Detailed list of what's included under this service umbrella
6. **Platform / Channel Expertise** — Logos + brief descriptions of platforms they work with
7. **Case Studies** — 2–3 client success stories with specific ROI numbers
8. **Why Disruptive** — Agency differentiators, philosophy (e.g., "we only take on clients we can actually help")
9. **Client Testimonials** — 3–5 detailed quotes with photos
10. **FAQ** — 6–8 accordion questions
11. **Final CTA** — "Get Your Free Audit" — same low-friction offer as the hero

**Standout patterns:**
- **"Free Audit" CTA** appears at the top AND bottom of every page. This is a genius low-commitment entry point that generates leads without requiring a full sales conversation.
- **Empathetic problem framing** — they name the specific frustrations their clients have before selling anything. This creates immediate resonance.
- **"We only work with clients we can help"** positioning is used throughout — signals quality over volume.
- Pages feel less like marketing copy and more like a direct pitch to a specific buyer persona.
- Each category page links to its sub-service pages, creating a very clean hierarchy.

#### Blog Structure

Disruptive's blog is their strongest content asset. Categories:

| Category | Focus |
|---|---|
| **PPC / Paid Search** | Google Ads tactics, bidding, campaign structure |
| **Paid Social** | Facebook/Instagram/TikTok ad strategies |
| **Conversion Rate Optimization** | CRO principles, landing page testing |
| **SEO** | Technical SEO, content strategy |
| **Social Media** | Organic social, platform updates |
| **Business** | Agency comparisons, vendor guides ("Best PPC Agencies") |
| **Marketing** | Broader digital marketing strategy |
| **News** | Company awards, rankings |
| **Giving Back** | Charitable initiatives (brand building) |

Disruptive's content strategy is smart: they publish a lot of "Best [Agency Type] in [City/Country]" roundup posts where they rank themselves. This captures high-intent search traffic from businesses actively evaluating agencies.

## Part 2: Aurelius Media — Current State Analysis

### Current Service Architecture

| Category | Services |
|---|---|
| **Performance Marketing** | Marketing Strategy Audit, Google Ads Management, Meta Ads Management |
| **Creative & Content** | AI Creative & Design, Creative & Design, Reels Ads Editing |
| **Growth & Automation** | AI Automation & Agents, No-Code App Development, AI Workshops & Training |
| **Industry Verticals** | Book & Author Marketing, Education & EdTech Marketing, Real Estate Marketing |

**Current service page template (9 sections):**
1. Hero (headline, description, CTA, hero image)
2. The Problem (pain points grid, 4–6 cards)
3. Our Approach (narrative text + bullet points)
4. What's Included (deliverables grid)
5. Results & Case Studies ⚠️ *currently placeholder*
6. Client Testimonial (single quote)
7. FAQ (accordion)
8. Related Services (3 cards)
9. Final CTA

### What's Working

- **Problem-first framing** in Section 2 is effective — mirrors Disruptive's empathetic approach
- **Deliverables grid** (Section 4) is concrete and confidence-building
- **Testimonials per service** are a nice touch and personalize each page
- **Related services** cross-linking is good for both UX and SEO
- Clean, dark aesthetic is distinctive in a market of generic blue/white agency sites

### Gaps to Address

1. **No social proof bar under the hero** — no agency-level numbers visible on service pages
2. **Results section is a placeholder** — weakest part of the template; needs real case studies
3. **Only one testimonial** per page — single data point is lower trust than multiple
4. **No low-commitment CTA** — only "Book a Strategy Call" (high commitment); no free audit or assessment offer
5. **Approach section** is text-heavy — could be more visual with numbered process steps
6. **No SEO service** — all three reference agencies offer SEO as a core service; Aurelius currently has no presence in this category
7. **Navigation is flat** — currently just "Services" with no category dropdown; harder for users to self-identify

## Part 3: Recommended Service Architecture

### Revised Navigation Structure: "What We Do"

Adopt Disruptive Advertising's category-based mega-menu model, adapted for Aurelius's unique AI positioning:

```
WHAT WE DO
├── Paid Search
│   ├── Google Ads Management
│   └── Marketing Strategy Audit
├── Paid Social
│   └── Meta Ads Management (Facebook & Instagram)
├── Creative
│   ├── AI Creative & Design
│   ├── Creative & Design
│   └── Reels & Video Editing
├── AI & Automation  ← Unique differentiator (no competitor has this)
│   ├── AI Automation & Agents
│   ├── No-Code App Development
│   └── AI Workshops & Training
└── Industry Solutions
    ├── Book & Author Marketing
    ├── Education & EdTech Marketing
    └── Real Estate Marketing
```

**Why this structure works for Aurelius:**
- Maps exactly to how clients think ("I need paid ads" → Paid Search/Social)
- Elevates **Creative** to a top-level category — right for an agency with strong creative output
- **AI & Automation** as a standalone category is a genuine market differentiator — no major competitor has this as a named service pillar
- **Industry Solutions** communicates specialization, not just service breadth
- Clean 5 categories with 2–3 items each — ideal for a mega-menu

### Services to Consider Adding (Future)

| Service | Rationale |
|---|---|
| **SEO** | All three reference agencies offer it; clients often ask about it alongside paid ads; creates organic pipeline |
| **LinkedIn Ads** | Natural expansion of Paid Social; strong B2B signal |
| **TikTok Ads** | Growing rapidly; natural fit with Reels editing capability |
| **Email / Lifecycle Marketing** | Disruptive has this as its own category; high client retention value |

## Part 4: Recommended Service Page Template

The current 9-section template is a solid foundation. The following changes make it competitive with the best in class:

### Revised Template (12 sections)

```
1.  HERO
2.  SOCIAL PROOF BAR         ← NEW
3.  THE PROBLEM
4.  OUR APPROACH (Visual)    ← UPGRADED
5.  WHAT'S INCLUDED
6.  PROCESS STEPS            ← NEW
7.  RESULTS & CASE STUDIES   ← UPGRADED (fill in real data)
8.  CLIENT TESTIMONIALS      ← UPGRADED (2-3 quotes)
9.  PLATFORMS & TOOLS        ← NEW (for relevant services)
10. FAQ
11. RELATED SERVICES
12. FINAL CTA (dual CTAs)    ← UPGRADED
```

#### Section-by-Section Guidance

**Section 1: Hero**
- Keep current structure (headline, description, hero image, CTA)
- Add a secondary CTA alongside "Book a Strategy Call" — something lower commitment like **"Get a Free Audit"** or **"See Our Work"**
- Consider making the headline more outcome-specific per service (e.g., "Scale Your Revenue with Google Ads That Actually Convert" vs. generic)

**Section 2: Social Proof Bar** *(new)*
- Agency-level stats only: e.g., "100,000+ copies sold for clients · 250% enrollment increase · 40+ buyer leads/week"
- Show 3–4 numbers that feel concrete and earned
- Thin bar, full-width, subtle styling — similar to WebFX's stat strip
- This addresses the biggest gap: no visible proof on landing

**Section 3: The Problem**
- Keep the current card grid — it's working well
- Slightly increase the specificity of pain points per service (make them feel like you're describing a real person's problem, not a generic category)

**Section 4: Our Approach** *(upgraded)*
- Replace the current text-heavy format with **3–5 numbered process steps**, each with:
  - Step number
  - Step name (short, action-oriented: "Audit → Strategize → Launch → Optimize")
  - 1–2 sentence description
- Keep the explanatory paragraph, but put it above the steps as intro copy
- This makes the process scannable and gives prospects a mental model of what working with you looks like

**Section 5: What's Included**
- Keep the deliverables grid — it's one of the strongest sections
- Consider grouping deliverables under 2–3 sub-headings (e.g., "Strategy," "Execution," "Reporting") to create more visual structure for services with 8+ deliverables

**Section 6: Process Steps** *(new, optional)*
- For services where the delivery process is multi-phase (e.g., Google Ads: Audit → Setup → Launch → Optimize → Report), a dedicated visual process section builds confidence
- Can be a horizontal timeline or vertical accordion
- Skip for simpler services where Section 4 already covers this

**Section 7: Results & Case Studies** *(upgraded — highest priority)*
- This is the weakest section currently (placeholder text)
- Minimum viable: 2–3 result callouts per service with:
  - Client industry (not necessarily name)
  - The challenge they faced
  - The specific result (with a real number)
  - Example: *"Education client saw 250% enrollment increase in 90 days"*
- Ideal: One mini case study per service page (2–3 paragraphs, with a stat headline)
- This section has more conversion impact than almost anything else on the page

**Section 8: Client Testimonials** *(upgraded)*
- Current template has one testimonial — increase to **2–3 per service page**
- For services where you only have one strong quote, use a broader "what clients say about us" section showing 2–3 quotes from different services (still themed around quality/results)
- Consider showing the testimonial photo more prominently (larger avatar)

**Section 9: Platforms & Tools** *(new, for relevant services)*
- For services like Google Ads, Meta Ads, AI Automation — show the platforms/tools you work with as logo badges
- Example for AI Automation: Claude, GPT-4, n8n, Zapier, Make
- Example for Google Ads: Google Ads, Google Analytics 4, Google Merchant Center, YouTube
- This signals technical credibility and helps prospects see "yes, they know the tools I care about"
- Only include for technical services where platform expertise matters

**Section 10: FAQ**
- Keep current structure — it's solid
- Aim for 6–8 questions per service (currently 4–5 on most)
- Add a question about pricing/investment on every page (e.g., "How much does Google Ads management cost?") — this is the most googled question for any service

**Section 11: Related Services**
- Keep the 3-card grid — good for internal linking and reducing bounce

**Section 12: Final CTA** *(upgraded — dual CTA)*
- Primary: "Book a 15-Minute Strategy Call" (current)
- Secondary: "Get a Free Audit" or "View Our Work" (lower commitment)
- Including two CTAs captures both buyer types: ready-to-buy and still-evaluating
- This is the most important UX change you can make for lead volume

## Part 5: Blog Structure Recommendations

### Recommended Blog Categories for Aurelius

| Category | Topics to Cover | SEO Opportunity |
|---|---|---|
| **Paid Advertising** | Google Ads tips, Meta Ads strategy, campaign structures, ROAS optimization, ad creative testing | "Google Ads for [industry]", "meta ads strategy 2025" |
| **AI & Marketing** | AI tools for marketers, prompt engineering for ads, AI automation workflows, ChatGPT for marketing | "AI marketing tools", "marketing automation with AI" |
| **Creative Strategy** | Ad creative best practices, hook formulas for Reels, design for conversion, video trends | "best performing ad creatives", "reel ads examples" |
| **Industry Insights** | Book marketing, education marketing, real estate lead gen — each as a dedicated category | "book launch marketing", "real estate lead generation" |
| **Results & Case Studies** | Client wins, campaign breakdowns, before/after analyses | Brand + trust building |
| **Agency News** | Rankings, team updates, tools we're using, client partnerships | — |

### Blog Post Content Strategy

Learning from Disruptive's most-trafficked content pattern:

1. **"Best [Service Type] Agencies"** roundups — Disruptive ranks themselves in lists like "Best PPC Agencies in the US." This is high-intent traffic from evaluating buyers. Aurelius should publish:
   - "Best Book Marketing Agencies in 2025"
   - "Best Education Marketing Agencies for Schools"
   - "Best Google Ads Agencies for [City/Region]"

2. **Service education posts** that link to service pages:
   - "How to Structure a Google Ads Campaign for Maximum ROAS" → links to Google Ads page
   - "AI-Generated Ad Creatives: What Works and What Doesn't" → links to AI Creative page

3. **AI-focused content** — this is a genuine content whitespace. All three reference agencies produce almost zero content about AI for marketing. Aurelius has a real opportunity to own this category:
   - "How to Use AI to 10x Your Ad Creative Output"
   - "5 AI Marketing Automations You Can Set Up This Week"
   - "The Complete Guide to AI Agents for Marketing Teams"
