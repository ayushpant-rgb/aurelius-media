## Part 1: Disruptive Advertising — Service Page Template

Every single Disruptive service page follows this exact template. The structure is nearly word-for-word the same between PPC, Paid Social, SEO, CRO, Creative, Lifecycle Marketing, and Analytics.

---

### Section 1 — Hero (H1)

**Format:** Problem stat headline, all-caps, short

**Examples:**
- PPC: *"76% OF AD SPEND GOES TO WASTE"*
- Paid Social: *"THE SOCIAL MEDIA MARKETING AGENCY THAT TURNS CLICKS INTO CASH"*
- SEO: *"GET SEO SERVICES THAT DRIVE RESULTS"*
- CRO: *"WE'RE THE CONVERSION RATE OPTIMIZATION AGENCY THAT MAKES YOU MONEY"*
- Creative: *"CREATIVE SERVICES AGENCY BUILT FOR RESULTS, NOT JUST DESIGN"*
- Lifecycle/Email: *"BOOST REVENUE AND RETENTION WITH THE BEST LIFECYCLE MARKETING AGENCY"*
- Analytics: *"THE ANALYTICS AGENCY THAT GIVES YOU DATA YOU CAN TRUST"*

**Pattern:** The best ones (PPC, Paid Social, CRO) lead with a problem or outcome. The weaker ones (SEO, Analytics) just use "agency that does X."

**Subheadline copy:** One sentence under the H1 — states the agency positioning + low-commitment hook. Example for PPC: *"The PPC Agency that puts more dollars in your pocket. After auditing thousands of ad accounts, we know how to help you outsmart, not outspend, your competition. And whether you work with us or not, we'll help you fix it."*

**Hero CTA:** `GET YOUR FREE AUDIT` — appears immediately under the H1/subhead.

---

### Section 2 — Audit Value Proposition (H2: "What do you get in the audit?")

This is Disruptive's **most important section**. It appears second, before any service description.

**Copy pattern:** *"Other agencies will tell you to spend more. We'll show you how to spend smarter. Our audit gives you honest, actionable insights that will help your business grow."*

**Format:** Numbered 3-point list:
1. Get the Facts — what they audit + what they find
2. Power Up Your Strategy — what the plan delivers
3. Take Action — the kicker: *"Whether you work with us or not, you'll walk away with a clear roadmap"*

This "whether you work with us or not" line is genius — it removes sales pressure entirely and makes the audit feel genuinely generous.

**Followed by:** One detailed client testimonial with photo, name, title, and a specific outcome quote.

---

### Section 3 — Social Proof Wall (H2: "We've helped thousands of business owners...")

**Format:** 3 short client quotes side by side, each with photo, name, role. Quotes are **outcome-focused** ("excellent ROI", "the only company I use", "very knowledgeable").

No case study detail here — that comes later. These are brand-trust quotes only.

---

### Section 4 — Methodology Trifecta (H2: EXECUTION / STRATEGY / VISION)

This is Disruptive's **signature framework** — the most distinctive content on their pages.

Each of the three pillars follows this exact pattern:
- **H2:** `[PILLAR]:`
- **Subheadline:** "MOST BUSINESSES COME TO US WITH A [PILLAR] PROBLEM"
- **2-paragraph copy:** Names the specific problem → explains how Disruptive solves it
- **Mid-pillar audit CTA:** embedded once more

**PPC Examples:**
- *Execution:* "Most brands don't have an execution problem — they have a **wasted ad spend** problem. High CPC, weak targeting, and money-burning campaigns are the real culprits."
- *Strategy:* "Most chase traffic—we chase **results**. We focus on the metrics that truly matter: customer acquisition cost, lifetime value, and return on ad spend."
- *Vision:* "If your brand lacks vision, you're not targeting growth—you're just hoping for a miracle."

**Why this works:** It positions Disruptive as a diagnostician, not a vendor. They're not selling you a service — they're identifying your problem type.

---

### Section 5 — Service Offerings (H2: "How our [service] can help")

Engagement tiers as H3s:

| H3 | Description |
|---|---|
| **DO IT YOURSELF** | Audit + roadmap only — you execute |
| **DO IT WITH US** | Strategy + coaching — you run it with their guidance |
| **DONE FOR YOU** | Full-service management |

This three-tier model is powerful — it captures clients at every readiness level and price point. No competitor structures their services this way. It also makes pricing feel flexible rather than fixed.

---

### Section 6 — Case Studies (H3 inline)

**Format:** Two inline case studies, each as:
- H3: `[CLIENT NAME]: [METRIC RESULT]`
- Brief description under

**Examples:**
- *Crossover Symmetry: 158% Revenue Growth*
- *Standartpark: 97% Revenue Increase*
- *NANI SWIMWEAR: 510% Revenue Growth*
- *Matterport: 38% Overall Improvement*
- *Heart 2 Heart: 4X Increase in CTR*

These are short, stat-first, no long story. The metric is the hook.

---

### Section 7 — Agency Differentiator (H2: "The [service] agency that gets you / proven track record")

Short paragraph of brand positioning. Usually 100–150 words. Ends with another audit CTA.

---

### Section 8 — Cross-Sell (H3: "Businesses who come to us for [service] also want help with")

**Format:** Horizontal list of 4–6 related service links. Used on every page consistently.

Examples for PPC page:
- Paid Social, CRO, Creative Services, Lifecycle Marketing, Analytics

This is their internal linking strategy — every service page links to 5 others.

---

### Section 9 — Blog Feed (H2: "Our latest content on [service]")

3 recent blog post cards at the bottom. Service-specific. Links to blog posts but they're presented as a value resource, not a navigation element.

## Part 2: Thrive Agency — Service Page Template

Thrive's template is longer, more SEO-optimized, and heavier on definitional content. Their pages are built to rank for informational keywords ("what is CRO", "what is social media marketing") while also converting.

---

### Section 1 — Intro Hook (H3, not H1)

Interestingly, Thrive's H1 doesn't render in the main content (it's probably in a hero image). The first visible text is an **H3 empathy statement**.

**Format:** Named "An [Service] Company That Gets It" (or similar)

**Copy pattern:** *"Let's face it. You know what you need, but you just don't know how to get it."*

Followed by a **bulleted pain-point list** — 4 specific client pain points phrased as first-person statements:
- *"Your website isn't being found online."*
- *"Your website traffic has diminished and you don't know why."*
- *"Your volume of incoming calls and leads has declined."*
- *"You're starting a new website and want to ramp up new business fast."*

This is very effective. It's specific, it mirrors the prospect's exact inner monologue.

**Followed by:** Video embed + review badge row (Google 150+, Clutch 100+, UpCity 50+)

---

### Section 2 — Case Studies Block (H2: "Providing [Service] Solutions to Solve Your Problems")

Thrive puts their **3 best case studies immediately after the intro** — this is their strongest pattern.

**Format per case study:**
- H2: `[CLIENT NAME]` (linked to case study)
- H2: `+800%` (metric 1 as standalone heading — e.g., "Online Leads")
- H2: `+126.5%` (metric 2 — e.g., "Organic Search Traffic")
- Italic client quote
- Read More link

The metric numbers as H2s is visually distinctive — big, bold numbers that immediately establish credibility.

---

### Section 3 — CTA Bridge (H2: "You've Finally Found the Right Agency With Answers")

Short urgency paragraph. Single CTA button. Creates a checkpoint before the long educational content that follows.

---

### Section 4 — Proprietary Tools (H2: "Thrive's Proprietary Tools That Power Smarter [Service]")

This section appears on **every** Thrive service page. It's their technology differentiator, equivalent to WebFX's RevenueCloudFX.

**Tools featured:**
- **Thrive Score** — diagnostic tool that evaluates 150+ marketing factors
- **ThriveAI** — AI suite exclusive to Thrive clients
- **Thrive Stats** — performance dashboard
- **Thrive Local** — local SEO & review management

Each tool gets: H3 title, 1–2 sentence description, link to tool page.

---

### Section 5 — Aggregate Social Proof Numbers (H2 stat callouts)

Large numbers as H2 headings on the SEO page:
- `11,886,819` — organic clicks driven
- `$267,484,503.72` — revenue generated
- `$91,230,012.21` — tracked value

Shown as three side-by-side stat blocks. The specificity (cents in the dollar figure) makes these feel extremely real.

---

### Section 6 — Service List (H2: "Our Professional [Service] Services")

H3 for each sub-service. Examples for SEO:
- Keyword Research & Strategy
- Link Building
- Technical SEO
- On-Page SEO
- Local SEO
- Content SEO

Short 1–3 sentence description per service. Usually 8–12 sub-services listed.

---

### Section 7 — Differentiators (H2: "Why Thrive's [Service] Are Better Than the Rest")

3–5 differentiator blocks, each with: H3 header + 50–100 word description.

---

### Section 8 — Deliverables (H2: "Services You Can Expect from Thrive")

More granular than Section 6 — this lists specific deliverables not just service categories. Very similar to Aurelius's current "What's Included" section.

---

### Section 9 — Process (H2: "Thrive's Agile [Service] Methodology")

Numbered process steps. This is the "how we work" section. Usually 5–7 steps.

---

### Section 10 — Trust / Ethics Signal

**Thrive SEO only:** "Thrive's SEO Code of Ethics" — a distinct, unusual section that signals they won't use black-hat tactics. Strong trust differentiator.

---

### Section 11 — FAQ

Accordion. 6–10 questions. SEO-keyword-rich questions ("What is X?", "How does X work?", "How much does X cost?").

## Part 3: Side-by-Side Template Comparison

| Section | Disruptive | Thrive | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero H1 | Problem stat, all-caps | Hidden in image | Disruptive's is much stronger |
| First CTA | Free Audit (Section 2) | "Boost My Visibility" (Section 3) | Disruptive's appears earlier |
| Case studies | 2, inline mid-page | 3, at the top with metric H2s | Thrive's position is better |
| Social proof | 3 quotes + 1 detailed | Review platform badges | Different approaches |
| Methodology | EXECUTION/STRATEGY/VISION | "Agile Methodology" steps | Disruptive's is more distinctive |
| Proprietary tech | None explicitly | ThriveScore, ThriveAI | Thrive wins here |
| Service list | 3 tiers (DIY/DWU/DFY) | 8–12 sub-services listed | Entirely different approaches |
| Definitional content | None | Heavy ("What is X?") | Thrive for SEO, Disruptive for conversion |
| Cross-sell | "Also want help with" H3 | Related services block | Both effective |
| Blog content | Integrated H2 section | Not integrated | Disruptive is smarter here |
| FAQ | Not present | Yes, every page | Thrive wins |
| Word count | ~1,000 | 4,500–6,500 | Thrive ranks higher, Disruptive converts faster |

## Part 4: Aurelius Service Page — Recommended Template

Based on the live crawl, here is the optimal template for Aurelius — combining Disruptive's **conversion structure** with Thrive's **SEO depth**, adapted to Aurelius's dark luxury brand.

Target length: **2,500–3,500 words**. Long enough to rank; short enough to not lose the reader.

---

```
SECTION 1 — HERO
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• H1: Problem-stat or outcome headline (Disruptive-style, not generic)
  Bad:  "Google Ads Management"
  Good: "Most Google Ads Budgets Are Burning. Yours Doesn't Have To."
• Subheadline: 1 sentence — Aurelius positioning + low-commitment hook
• Dual CTA: Primary "Book a 15-Min Strategy Call" + Secondary "Get a Free Audit"
• Hero image/shader

SECTION 2 — SOCIAL PROOF BAR
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• 3–4 agency-level stats (Thrive-style specificity)
  e.g.: "100K+ Copies Sold for Authors · 250% Enrollment Increase ·
        40+ Qualified Leads/Week for Real Estate Clients"
• Review platform badges (when you have them)

SECTION 3 — PAIN POINTS (THE PROBLEM)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• H2: "[Empathy statement]" (e.g., "Most [service] Agencies Don't Solve the Real Problem")
• 4–6 bullet pain points in first person (Thrive-style — mirror the prospect's internal monologue)
• Keep existing card grid — it works well

SECTION 4 — CASE STUDIES (RESULTS FIRST)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Move case studies UP — show them before the approach (like Thrive)
• Format: Client industry + metric callout (large) + 1-sentence quote
• Minimum 2, ideally 3 per service page
• This is the highest-impact missing section right now

SECTION 5 — OUR APPROACH (METHODOLOGY)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• H2: "Our [Service] Framework" or "How We Think About [Service]"
• Adopt a named Aurelius framework (equivalent to Disruptive's Execution/Strategy/Vision)
  Suggested: "DIAGNOSE → ARCHITECT → LAUNCH → OPTIMIZE"
  Or for AI services: "AUDIT → AUTOMATE → AMPLIFY"
• 3–4 pillars, each with: pillar name + problem it solves + how Aurelius fixes it
• This creates a distinctive methodology that no competitor has

SECTION 6 — WHAT'S INCLUDED (DELIVERABLES)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Keep existing deliverables grid — it's strong
• Group into 2–3 sub-categories for services with 8+ deliverables:
  "Strategy", "Execution", "Reporting"

SECTION 7 — ENGAGEMENT MODEL (3 TIERS)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Borrow Disruptive's DO IT YOURSELF / DO IT WITH US / DONE FOR YOU structure
• Rename for Aurelius: "AUDIT ONLY / GUIDED / FULL-SERVICE"
• Each tier: what's included, who it's for, CTA
• This is missing from Aurelius entirely and captures clients at different budgets

SECTION 8 — PLATFORMS & TOOLS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Logo badge row for relevant services only
• Google Ads: Google Ads, GA4, Google Merchant Center, YouTube
• Meta: Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp Business
• AI services: Claude, GPT-4, n8n, Make, Zapier, Midjourney
• Signals technical credibility without explaining it

SECTION 9 — TESTIMONIALS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Upgrade to 2–3 testimonials (currently 1)
• Mix: 1 outcome-specific quote + 1 relationship/process quote + 1 brief one-liner

SECTION 10 — FAQ
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Keep existing accordion
• Add: "How much does [service] cost?" (the most Googled question on any service page)
• Expand to 6–8 questions per page

SECTION 11 — RELATED SERVICES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Keep existing 3-card grid
• Label it "Clients who need [service] also explore:" (mirrors Disruptive's language)

SECTION 12 — FINAL CTA
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Same dual CTA as hero: "Book a Call" + "Get a Free Audit"
• Add a 1-sentence urgency line: "Most clients see their first results within 30 days."
```

## Part 6: The Single Biggest Takeaway

**Disruptive's free audit CTA beats everything.**

It appears in: Hero, end of audit section, after the EXECUTION section, after STRATEGY, after VISION, and after the agency differentiator. That's 6 times on a ~1,000-word page.

It works because:
1. It's low-commitment — no sales call required
2. It provides genuine value — a real audit of their account
3. The copy removes pressure — "whether you work with us or not"
4. It creates a lead-capture moment before the prospect has to decide anything

Aurelius's equivalent: **"Get a Free Marketing Audit"** — a 30-minute Loom or PDF review of the prospect's current paid ads, creative, or automation setup. This is the single highest-impact CTA change you can make.
