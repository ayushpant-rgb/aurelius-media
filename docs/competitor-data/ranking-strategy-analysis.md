# Competitor Ranking Strategy Analysis
### Aurelius Media — Live Data (March 2026)
### Sites: Disruptive Advertising (1,608 URLs) · Thrive Agency (2,394 URLs)

---

## Executive Summary

Disruptive and Thrive use fundamentally different ranking playbooks. Thrive is a **quantity and intent-capture machine** — they publish "Best [X] Agency" roundups for every niche and city and rank themselves #1 in each one. Disruptive is an **authority and depth machine** — they've built the internet's most comprehensive PPC/CRO content library organized in topic clusters. Both work, but through entirely different mechanisms.

Aurelius's whitespace sits in the gap between them: **AI marketing content** — an emerging keyword category that neither competitor owns, where Aurelius has a genuine positioning advantage.

## Part 1: Thrive Agency — Ranking Strategy

### The Core Playbook: "Best Of" Roundups + Location Pages

Thrive's entire content strategy is built around two high-intent query types:

**1. "Best [Service] Agency" Posts — 164 pages**

These target the highest-intent searches in the buyer journey: someone who has already decided they need an agency and is now evaluating options. Thrive publishes a roundup, ranks themselves #1 in their own list, and captures the traffic.

**Template (confirmed from live scrape of "20 Best Digital Marketing Agencies in 2026"):**
- H1: `[N] Best [Service Type] in [Year]`
- Table of Contents with all agencies named (ranking signals via anchor links)
- Intro: 2–3 paragraphs on what to look for in an agency
- Body: 20 entries, each: Agency name + H3, 150–300 word description, services listed, who they're best for
- **Thrive is always #1 or #2 on their own list**
- Ends with: "Partner With Thrive" CTA + FAQ section
- Length: 3,000–6,000 words
- Author byline, published date, read time — all present (E-E-A-T signals)

**Sample post titles covering every niche:**
```
"20 Best Digital Marketing Agencies in 2026"
"11 Best Dental Digital Marketing Agencies in 2026"
"11 Best San Francisco SEO Companies in 2026"
"Best Hotel Marketing Agencies"
"Best Roofing Marketing Companies"
"Best HVAC Marketing Companies"
"Best Franchise Digital Marketing Agencies"
"Best Manufacturing SEO Agencies"
"Best Medical Digital Marketing Companies"
"Best AI SEO Agencies in 2026"
"Best Retail SEO Companies"
"Best Industrial SEO Companies"
"Best Garage Door Repair SEO Companies"
"Best Ecommerce Marketing Companies"
"Best Amazon Marketing Companies"
"Best Texas SEO Companies"
"Best Austin SEO Companies"
"Best Dallas SEO Companies"
"Best Houston SEO Companies"
"Best Miami SEO Companies"
```

They publish city-specific AND niche-specific variants of the same pattern. This is programmatic content at scale — the underlying template barely changes.

**Why this ranks:**
- Query intent is "I want to hire someone right now" — Google rewards pages that directly answer this
- Long-form (3,000–6,000 words) with structured data
- High internal link equity from their 1,700+ page site
- Each post mentions 10–20 competitor brand names, so they also capture competitor-branded searches

---

**2. Location Pages — 83 pages**

Thrive targets every major US city with a dedicated landing page for each service in that city.

**URL pattern:**
```
/dallas-seo-company/
/austin-ppc-agency/
/san-antonio-digital-marketing-agency/
/houston-ppc-agency/
/chicago-seo-company/
/chicago-web-design-company/
/san-antonio-web-design-company/
/frisco-digital-marketing-agency/
/syracuse-digital-marketing-agency/
```

**Template (confirmed from live scrape of `/dallas-seo-company/`):**
- H1: `Award Winning [City] [Service] Company`
- 2-sentence agency intro mentioning the city by name
- **Local flavor paragraph** — 2–3 sentences about the city (landmarks, economic scene, industry)
- 2–3 paragraphs of service description
- "GET MY FREE PROPOSAL" form embedded early
- Phone number
- "HOW IT WORKS" 3-step process
- Review platform badges (Google 150+, Clutch 50+, UpCity 50+)
- Full service detail section
- Client results section
- FAQ with city-specific questions

**Why this ranks:** Google's local algorithm heavily favors pages that mention the specific city name, have NAP (name/address/phone), and have relevant content. Each city page is a standalone landing page, not just a template swap — the local paragraph is customized.

---

**3. How-To Content — 419 pages**

Thrive's third pillar. Posts targeting informational queries with high-volume, low-competition keywords.

**Sample topics:**
- "How to Use ChatGPT for Keyword Research"
- "How to Determine if Your Brand Is Visible in AI Search Results"
- "How to Improve Your Site's Visibility in AI Results"
- "What Your Homepage Should Achieve for Your Website"

These posts are SEO-in → service-out: educational content that captures top-of-funnel traffic, then converts via inline CTAs back to service pages.

---

**4. Comparison Posts — 26 pages**

Targets decision-stage queries where prospects are evaluating options:
- "Hiring In-House vs. An SEO Agency"
- "Digital Marketing vs. Performance Marketing vs. Growth Marketing"
- "SearchGPT vs. Google: Which Should You Optimize For?"
- "Franchise SEO vs. Local SEO"
- "Facebook Custom Audiences vs. Lookalike Audiences"

These capture users who are close to a decision and still have an unresolved question.

No agency has built a real AI marketing content library. This is Aurelius's biggest opportunity — it matches the brand, matches the services, and the competition is almost zero right now.

## Part 7: What Aurelius Should NOT Do

**Do not try to replicate Thrive's volume play immediately.** Thrive has 1,781 blog posts. Building that takes years. Instead:

1. **Go narrow and deep** on AI marketing content — own one specific keyword category before trying to cover everything.
2. **Do not build location pages yet** — they require domain authority you don't have. Focus on content first.
3. **Do not publish thin content.** Thrive wins because their posts are 3,000–8,000 words. Aurelius's 4 current blog posts are around 1,200 words. Every new post should be 2,500+ words minimum.
4. **Do not skip internal linking.** Right now blog posts don't link back to service pages (flagged in PROJECT_CONTEXT.md). Every post must link to at least 2 service pages.
