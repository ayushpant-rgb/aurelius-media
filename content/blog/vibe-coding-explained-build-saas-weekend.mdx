A founder we work with in Bangalore messaged us in January. She had been sitting on a SaaS idea for two years because she could not afford a developer and did not want to learn to code. Six months later, she had a working MVP with 40 paying users. She built it herself in three weekends.

She is not an engineer. She is a marketing director.

This is not an isolated story anymore. Across our network, we are seeing founders, marketers, and operators ship real software using a workflow that the internet has started calling **vibe coding**.

Here is what it actually is — and whether it can work for you.

---

## What Is Vibe Coding?

Vibe coding is a style of software development where you describe what you want to build in plain English and use AI coding tools to generate, iterate, and debug the code for you. You are not learning syntax. You are not memorizing APIs. You are directing — like a film director who does not need to operate the camera.

The term was coined by OpenAI co-founder Andrej Karpathy, and it stuck because it captures the feel of the process: you are vibing with an AI collaborator, moving fast, following your intuition, building toward something rather than engineering toward something.

**The tools making this possible:**
- **Cursor** — an AI-native code editor that understands your entire codebase and can write, edit, and debug code based on natural language instructions
- **Bolt.new** — browser-based, generates full-stack apps from a single prompt, no setup required
- **Replit** — cloud development environment with built-in AI that can spin up and deploy apps rapidly
- **v0 (Vercel)** — specializes in generating React UI components from descriptions

The numbers back up the shift: 92% of US developers now use AI coding tools daily. 41% of all code written in 2025 was AI-generated. This is not a fringe movement. It is the new default.

## What You Can Actually Build

Let me be concrete. Here is what is genuinely buildable via vibe coding with no prior programming experience:

**Simple SaaS tools**
- Internal dashboards for tracking KPIs
- Lead scoring calculators for your sales team
- Client-facing portals for delivering reports
- Simple CRM-like tools for niche use cases

**Landing pages and micro-sites**
- High-converting product landing pages
- Event registration and waitlist pages
- Portfolio or case study showcase sites

**Automation interfaces**
- Form-to-database tools
- Simple booking or scheduling tools
- Internal tools that connect to APIs (Notion, Airtable, Slack)

**MVP-level products**
- Simple marketplaces
- Directory websites
- Tool-as-a-service with a single core function

This is where the "weekend SaaS" story becomes real. If your idea has a simple, well-defined core function, a working version is achievable faster than you think.

## What You Cannot Skip

Here is the part most vibe coding tutorials conveniently leave out.

**You still need to think like a product person.** AI can write code. It cannot decide what your product should do, how it should handle edge cases, or what the user experience should feel like. The quality of your output is entirely determined by the quality of your input. If your instructions are vague, the product will be vague.

**You will hit walls.** When an AI-generated codebase gets complex — when you are adding a third feature to a product that already has two, when you need to integrate a payment system, when something breaks and the error message makes no sense — vibe coding slows down dramatically. The 91% of AI-generated code that works is great. The 9% that does not can consume hours.

**Security and production-readiness require expertise.** An MVP built via vibe coding that handles payments, stores user data, or integrates with third-party APIs needs a proper security review before it goes live with real users. This is not optional.

**You are building on templates, not architecture.** Vibe-coded products often accumulate technical debt quickly. They are fast to start and expensive to scale if the foundation was not designed with growth in mind.

## The Non-Technical Founder's Honest Assessment

We have built multiple MVPs using AI-assisted coding workflows at Aurelius Media — including parts of this website. Here is our honest take:

**Vibe coding is genuinely transformative for:**
- Validating an idea before spending money on a developer
- Building internal tools that would otherwise sit on a backlog forever
- Moving from "I wish I had a tool that did X" to "I have a tool that does X" in days
- Creating simple client-facing deliverables with a digital component

**Vibe coding is a starting point, not an ending point for:**
- Anything that handles real user data at scale
- Products that need to be production-hardened for enterprise clients
- Systems that require complex integrations or custom APIs
- Anything where downtime or bugs have serious business consequences

The honest framing: vibe coding collapses the cost and time to reach *version 0.1*. Getting from 0.1 to 1.0 — a product that reliably serves real customers — still requires real engineering judgment, whether that comes from you learning more, hiring someone, or working with an agency that can bridge the gap.

## How to Actually Start This Weekend

If you have an idea and want to test vibe coding, here is the simplest possible path:

**Step 1: Define the one thing your product does**
Write a single sentence: "This tool lets [user] do [specific thing] by [mechanism]." If you cannot write this sentence, you are not ready to build yet.

**Step 2: Start with Bolt.new**
Go to bolt.new. Type your description. Watch it generate a working interface. Do not overthink the first version. Just see what comes out.

**Step 3: Iterate with specificity**
The most effective prompts are specific: "Add a button in the top right corner that exports this table to CSV" rather than "make the export better." Treat the AI like a junior developer who is talented but needs clear instructions.

**Step 4: Deploy immediately**
Vercel and Netlify offer one-click deploys that are free for small projects. Get your MVP live — even if only you and two friends can see it — and start getting feedback from reality rather than from your imagination.

**Step 5: Know when to hand off**
When your vibe-coded product is getting real users and you start hitting walls, that is not a failure. That is product-market validation. That is exactly when you should bring in professional development support to build the real version.

## The Bigger Picture

Vibe coding is democratizing software creation in a way that is genuinely historic. The barriers between "I have an idea" and "I have a product" have never been lower.

But the most important thing it requires is the same thing it has always required: clarity of thought. What problem are you solving? Who are you solving it for? What is the simplest version that proves the idea works?

Answer those questions well, and the tools will take you further than you imagined. Answer them vaguely, and you will build something that does not quite work and cannot quite explain why.
